About Kurdistan

Kurdistan is a beautiful region known for its rich history, stunning landscapes, and vibrant culture. Explore ancient cities, majestic mountains, and more!.
Kurdistan refers to an ethnically and culturally distinct region predominantly inhabited by Kurds, a mainly Kurdish-speaking people.
It is situated across parts of several countries in the Middle East, including southeastern Turkey, northern Iraq, western Iran, and northeastern Syria.
The Kurdish people have a long history and distinct cultural identity, including their own language, traditions, and customs.

Plan Your Trip

Plan your visit to Kurdistan with helpful information on accommodations, transportation, and more.

Day 1-2: Arrival in Erbil Arrival: Arrive at Erbil International Airport, the capital of Iraqi Kurdistan. Explore Erbil Citadel: Begin your journey by visiting the Erbil Citadel, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. Explore the narrow streets, museums, and historical sites within the citadel. Qaysari Bazaar: Wander through the bustling Qaysari Bazaar, where you can shop for souvenirs, spices, textiles, and local crafts. Shanidar Park: Spend some time relaxing at Shanidar Park, a beautiful green space located near the citadel.

Day 3-4: Sulaymaniyah
Travel to Sulaymaniyah: Take a scenic drive to Sulaymaniyah, the cultural capital of Iraqi Kurdistan. Sulaymaniyah Museum: Explore the Sulaymaniyah Museum, which houses an impressive collection of Kurdish artifacts, archaeological finds, and historical exhibits. Amna Suraka (Red Jail): Visit Amna Suraka, also known as the Red Jail, a former Baathist prison turned museum that provides insight into the Kurdish struggle against Saddam Hussein's regime. Bazian Pass and Goyje Bridge: Take a day trip to Bazian Pass and Goyje Bridge for breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Day 5-7: Dohuk and Surroundings
Travel to Dohuk: Drive to Dohuk, a picturesque city located near the Turkish and Syrian borders. Dohuk Bazaar: Explore the vibrant Dohuk Bazaar, known for its unique blend of Kurdish and Middle Eastern cultures. Akre: Take a day trip to Akre, a historic town known for its ancient citadel and picturesque surroundings. Zawa Mountain: Hike or drive up Zawa Mountain for panoramic views of Dohuk and the surrounding plains. Gali Ali Beg Waterfall: Visit Gali Ali Beg Waterfall, a stunning natural attraction located near the town of Dohuk.

Day 8-10: Rural Kurdistan
Rural Villages: Spend a few days exploring rural villages and experiencing Kurdish hospitality firsthand. Stay in guesthouses or homestays to immerse yourself in local culture and traditions. Outdoor Activities: Enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking, horseback riding, or picnicking in the countryside. Local Cuisine: Sample traditional Kurdish cuisine, including dishes like kebabs, dolma, and various types of flatbread. Cultural Experiences: Participate in cultural activities such as folk music and dance performances, cooking classes, or craft workshops.

Day 11-12: Return to Erbil
Return to Erbil: Travel back to Erbil for your departure. Last-Minute Explorations: Spend your last day in Kurdistan exploring any sights or attractions you may have missed, or simply relax and soak in the atmosphere of the city. Departure: Head to the airport for your onward journey, taking with you memories of an unforgettable trip to Kurdistan.
